Difference Amplifier PCB - Part No: 04A-015

This Printed Circuit Board (PCB) features a basic design of a difference amplifier. The board size is approximately 50 x 50 mm and there are 4 pieces per panel. The panel size is 100 x 100 mm, V-scored.

What Does This Circuit Do?

The difference amplifier is a type of electronic amplifier that amplifies the difference between two inputs but suppresses any voltage common to the two inputs. It is an integral part of many devices that involve analog signals.

How Does It Work?

The difference amplifier takes two inputs. When a voltage is applied to these inputs, the amplifier amplifies the difference in voltage between the two inputs. The output voltage is directly proportional to the difference between the input voltages.

Where Is It Used In Real Life?

Difference amplifiers are used in a wide variety of applications, including audio equipment, telecommunications devices, and scientific instruments such as oscilloscopes and signal generators.
