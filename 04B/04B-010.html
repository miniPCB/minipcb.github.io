AI Generated

The 04B-010 implements a unity‑gain buffer (emitter/source follower): the transistor collector/drain is tied to V+, and the emitter/source returns to GND through R4. C2 and C3 decouple the supply; TP5 and TP6 read V+ and GND. The input is AC‑coupled by C1 into a bias network R1–R3 (with trimmer R2) that sets base/gate DC level; TP4 observes this node. The output is taken from the emitter/source, AC‑coupled by C4; TP7 monitors the emitter/source DC, and TP8 monitors the AC‑coupled output. R5 provides a defined load for characterization.

Biasing: adjust R2 so the emitter/source (TP7) sits near mid‑rail for symmetric swing (Ve ≈ ~0.45–0.55·V+ for BJT; allow an extra VGS for MOSFETs). Make the R1–R3 divider 20–200 kΩ total. Lower values improve noise and PSRR but raise quiescent draw; higher values reduce current but increase susceptibility to bias drift and input leakage. Choose R4 between ~220 Ω and 2.2 kΩ: smaller values increase standing current, lower output impedance, and improve linearity into heavy loads, at the cost of dissipation; larger values save power but raise output impedance and distortion under load.

Coupling and decoupling: set C1 by the source impedance into the R1–R3 bias resistance so the input high‑pass corner fIN = 1/(2π·Req_in·C1) is well below the lowest signal frequency. Typical C1 is 100 nF–10 µF. Choose C4 by the load (R5 || external load): fOUT = 1/(2π·Rload·C4). Typical C4 is 10–470 µF. Use C2 ≈ 100 nF ceramic at the device and C3 ≈ 4.7–47 µF bulk; too little decoupling raises noise/oscillation risk, too much increases inrush.

  • R1–R3: 20–200 kΩ total; trade noise/PSRR vs. current draw and bias stability.
  • R4: 220 Ω–2.2 kΩ; trade output drive/linearity vs. dissipation.
  • R5: 1–100 kΩ; smaller stresses the follower and raises C4’s LF corner.
  • C1: 100 nF–10 µF; small = LF roll‑off, large = size/leakage.
  • C4: 10–470 µF; small = bass loss, large = start‑up thump/leakage.
  • C2/C3: ~0.1 µF + 4.7–47 µF near the device for stable rails.
